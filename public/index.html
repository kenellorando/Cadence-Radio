About Cadence:

Cadence is an online radio whose creation was inspired by the anime-themed R/a/dio. Originally started as an experiment in February 2017, the project is my first endeavour to practice a full range of IT-skills.

Cadence is served through a Python webserver interacting with a Postgres music database, both configured around a Liquidsoap/Icecast stream server. There is no frontend framework anywhere; everything you see was created using purely original code.

The server typically plays all the genres of music I like, a mix of classic rock, synthpop, and metal. I occasionally override the server and play a certain genre, artist, or the same song on an endless loop.

If you're interested in contributing to this project's development, everything is available on GitHub: source/issues.

Legal Disclaimer

Cadence Radio is a DMCA compliant, "non-commercial educational" (NCE) broadcast. As an NCE, Cadence Radio is non-profit and does not accept advertisements for its webpages or broadcasts.

9/26: Cadence 3.0.0

Today, I am pleased to announce the release of Cadence 3.0.0. This historical landmark in the development history of Cadence marks the successful integration of the new Python webserver, as well as the retirement of our first 2.0 Node.js server. You may notice improvements in audio access speed and reduced proneness to crashing.

Many thanks to the contributions of Ryan Hodin (za419), architect of these new back-end features and stability improvements. Many thanks to him for his dedication, and all our newcoming friends and developers who work towards a more rhythmic experience.
